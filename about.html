The first creators of our childhood.

MAGNAVOX – 1972

Ralph H. Baer.

The Magnavox Odyssey console was created because Ralph H. Baer had a vision of bringing interactive video games into the home. It was released in September 1972 to allow people to play games on their televisions for the first time.

It was a commercial success because it introduced the idea of home video games; although it wasn’t a massive success.

PS – 1994

Ken Kutaragi.

The first PlayStation console was released in Japan on December 3, 1994. It emerged after a failed deal between Sony and Nintendo to develop a CD-ROM peripheral for the Super Nintendo.

After the negotiations failed, Sony decided to create its own console, The Console, to compete in the 32-bit video game console market.

SEGA – 1960

David Rosen, Ray LeMaire, Richard Stewart.

Sega emerged from the arcade industry after World War II. Its history began in 1940 with Standard Games, founded in Hawaii to provide entertainment for American soldiers through coin-op machines on military bases.

The company later moved to Japan under the name Service Games of Japan, continuing its mission of providing entertainment to the armed forces in 1960.

Interesting facts about the first video game console in history

The world’s first home console.

The Magnavox Odyssey in 1972 was the first video game console in history. It had no sound and only displayed white squares on the screen. It was sold with plastic sheets that were placed over the television to simulate graphics and scenes.

What was special about PS1?

The console’s design prioritised 3D polygonal graphics. PlayStation was designed to be agile and inclusive, attracting the support of numerous third-party developers.
